<Title>Parliament—business of the House</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 22 July 1885 vol 299 c1517</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-07-22</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>113</WordCount>


<speaker>MR. JAMES STUART</speaker> asked, Whether it was the intention of the Government to bring on the Criminal Law Amendment Bill on Monday? 
<speaker>THE SECRETARY OF STATE FOB THE HOME DEPARTMENT (Sir R. ASSHETON CROSS)</speaker> said, he could say nothing about proceeding with the Bill, anxious as he was to press it on, until the House had disposed of the Medical Relief Bill , and probably also the Army Estimates. 
<speaker>MR. MONK</speaker> asked whether the Report on the Medical Relief Bill would be taken tomorrow? 
<speaker>THE SECRETARY OF STATE (Sir R. ASSHETOX CROSS)</speaker> said, it was better to say nothing about Business until they saw what progress was made today with the Irish Estimates.
