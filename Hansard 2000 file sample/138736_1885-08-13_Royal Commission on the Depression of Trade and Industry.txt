<Title>Royal Commission on the Depression of Trade and Industry</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 13 August 1885 vol 301 cc24-5</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-08-13</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>121</WordCount>


<speaker>MR. CAUSTON</speaker> I wish to ask the Chancellor of the Exchequer a Question in reference to the proposed Royal Commission on the Depression of Trade in consequence of his answer the other night as to the scope of the inquiry. I wish to ask him, as this Commission is not so much for the purpose of deciding or settling on a policy as to ascertain and collate facts, why this work could not have been undertaken by the officers of the Board of Trade, and considerable delay and expense saved? 
<speaker>THE CHANCELLOR OF THE EXCHEQUER</speaker> This work has not been done by the officers of the Board of Trade because we thought it would be better done by a Royal Commission.
