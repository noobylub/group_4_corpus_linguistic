( Sir Henry Holland, Mr. Attorney General. ) [BILL 216.] SECOND READING. Order for Second Reading read. 
<speaker>THE SECRETARY TO THE TREASURY (Sir HENRY HOLLAND)</speaker> said, that this was also a very simple Bill. It provided that the Charity Commissioners should, subject to the approval of the Court of Chancery, make a scheme for the distribution of part of the Pole-hampton Estate. Motion made, and Question, "That the Bill be now read a second time,"— ( Sir Henry Holland, )—put, and agreed to. Bill committed for Tomorrow. cc548-66 SUPPLY [8TH JULY] REPORT. 6,745 words
