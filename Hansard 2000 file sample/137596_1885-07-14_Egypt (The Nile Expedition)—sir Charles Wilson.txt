<Title>Egypt (The Nile Expedition)—sir Charles Wilson</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 14 July 1885 vol 299 c660</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-07-14</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>88</WordCount>


<speaker>MR. ARTHUR O'CONNOR</speaker> asked the Secretary of State for War, Why the letter or telegram of Lord Wolseley to Sir Charles Wilson, asking for explanations concerning his delays, was not included among the Papers presented to Parliament; and, why Sir Charles Wilson's reasons, which Lord Wolseley said "must speak for themselves," were not included in those Papers? 
<speaker>THE SECRETARY OF STATE (Mr. W. H. SMITH)</speaker> Lord Wolseley has not forwarded to the War Office any letter or telegram on this subject addressed by him to Sir Charles Wilson.
