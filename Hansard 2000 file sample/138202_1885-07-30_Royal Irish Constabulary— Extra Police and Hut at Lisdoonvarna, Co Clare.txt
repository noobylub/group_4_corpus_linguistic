<Title>Royal Irish Constabulary— Extra Police and Hut at Lisdoonvarna, Co Clare</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 30 July 1885 vol 300 c508</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-07-30</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>88</WordCount>


<speaker>MR. KENNY</speaker> asked the Chief Secretary to the Lord Lieutenant of Ireland, If the Government now propose to remove the hut and police from Kilmoon, near Lisdoonvarna, county Clare; and, if he is aware that no outrages have occurred in the district for a very considerable time, and that there is no apparent necessity for the retention of this constabulary post? 
<speaker>THE CHIEF SECRETARY (Sir WILLIAM HART DYKE)</speaker> It is contemplated to remove this hut in about a month's time, should the state of the locality continue peaceful.
