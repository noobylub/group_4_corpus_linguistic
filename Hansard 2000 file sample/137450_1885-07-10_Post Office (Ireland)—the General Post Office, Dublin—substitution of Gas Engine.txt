<Title>Post Office (Ireland)—the General Post Office, Dublin—substitution of Gas Engines for Steam</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 10 July 1885 vol 299 c278</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-07-10</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>144</WordCount>


<speaker>MR. SEXTON</speaker> asked the Postmaster General, Whether gas engines have been substituted for steam engines for the working of the pneumatic system in the General Post Office, Dublin; whether it is admitted that the steam engines gave satisfaction, and that the Technical Department and Mr. Preece, chief electrician to the Post Office, reported against the change; if this be so, why was the alteration made; was the work carried out by private tender, or was it publicly advertised; what was the cost of the alteration; how the new engines have been found to work; and, whether the cost of their maintenance is not greater than that of the steam engines? 
<speaker>THE POSTMASTER GENERAL (Lord JOHN MANNERS),</speaker> in reply, said, that the gas engines had been introduced by the Irish Office of Works, who paid the costs of maintenance, except the wages of the attendants.
