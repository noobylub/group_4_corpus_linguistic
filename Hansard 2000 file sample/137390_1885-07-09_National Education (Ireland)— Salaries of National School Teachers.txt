<Title>National Education (Ireland)— Salaries of National School Teachers</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 09 July 1885 vol 299 c119</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-07-09</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>99</WordCount>

MR. JUSTIN HUNTLYM'CARTHY asked the Chief Secretary to the Lord Lieutenant of Ireland, Whether the Government will grant a Supplementary Estimate, in addition to the class salaries of the Irish National School Teachers, pending legislation? THE CHIEF SECRETARY This is a very serious Question, and requires careful consideration. I am not in a position to give any undertaking in respect to it. I should, perhaps, observe that the Roman Catholic Bishops in their letter, which is now before the House, point to increased payment in the shape of results fees as the proper method of augmenting the teachers' salaries.
