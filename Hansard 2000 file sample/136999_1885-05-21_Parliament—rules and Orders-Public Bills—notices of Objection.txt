<Title>Parliament—rules and Orders-Public Bills—notices of Objection</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 21 May 1885 vol 298 cc1039-40</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-05-21</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>129</WordCount>


<speaker>MR. WARTON</speaker> desired to ask a Question with regard to the duration of Notices of objections to Bills which, according to an Order of the House, expired the week after that in which, they were given? 
<speaker>MR. SPEAKER</speaker> said, the Question could be put when the point arose. There was no Question now before the House. 
<speaker>MR. WARTON</speaker> said, he desired to put the Question on account of the Holidays. 
<speaker>MR. GIBSON</speaker> said, he would ask whether the matter was not settled by the late Speaker ruling that a Notice of objection given after the Motion for Adjournment had been agreed to was kept alive for the usual period after the Holidays? 
<speaker>MR. SPEAKER</speaker> said, the Notice would operate for the usual period after the meeting of the House.
