<Title>Tramways Order in Council (Ireland) Bill</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 30 July 1885 vol 300 c519</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-07-30</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>100</WordCount>


<speaker>COLONEL COLTHURST</speaker> asked the honourable Member for county Cavan, Whether he will withdraw his notice of opposition to the Tramways Order in Council (Ireland) Bill , so as to allow that measure to be discussed? 
<speaker>MR. BIGGAR,</speaker> in reply, said, he had never known a case in which there was so much anxiety to have a Bill defeated as in regard to this particular Bill; and he did not feel disposed to withdraw his Notice of opposition, and thus assist Sir George Colthurst, who was a nephew of the hon. and gallant Member for Cork County, to rob the ratepayers.
