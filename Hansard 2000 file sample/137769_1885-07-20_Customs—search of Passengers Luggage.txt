<Title>Customs—search of Passengers Luggage</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 20 July 1885 vol 299 c1191</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-07-20</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>60</WordCount>


<speaker>MR. O'SHEA</speaker> asked the Secretary of State, Whether he will take into consideration the possibility of relaxing the present vexatious system of search of passengers' luggage on arrival from the Continent? 
<speaker>THE SECRETARY OF STATE FOR THE HOME DEPARTMENT (Sir R. ASSHETON CROSS),</speaker> in reply, said, he was afraid it was impossible to make any alteration in the present regulations.
