<Title>The Electoral Acts— Distribution</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 11 August 1885 vol 300 cc1741-2</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-08-11</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>124</WordCount>


<speaker>SIR HENRY JAMES</speaker> asked the Secretary of State for the Home Department, Whether he would take steps to have the new Electoral Acts, the Franchise Act , the Redistribution Act , and the Registration Acts at once printed and distributed? Before the change of Government a promise was made that those Acts should be distributed at an early period, so as to be in the hands of all concerned before the General Election. 
<speaker>MR. BRODRICK</speaker> suggested that the new Corrupt Practices Act should also be issued. 
<speaker>THE SECRETARY OF STATE (Sir R. ASSHETON CROSS)</speaker> said, he should take care that was carried out as soon as possible. With regard to the further Question, the Corrupt Practices Act could be bought at the Queen's Printers.
