<Title>India—native States-Expulsion of a French Citizen From Travancore</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 23 July 1885 vol 299 cc1618-9</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-07-23</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>116</WordCount>


<speaker>SIR GEORGE CAMPBELL</speaker> asked the Secretary of State for India, Whether the India Office has any information regarding the expulsion from the Native State of Travancore of M. Montelar, a French citizen; and, if so, by what authority he was expelled, and why? 
<speaker>THE SECRETARY OF STATE (Lord RANDOLPH CHURCHILL)</speaker> , in reply, said, that the matter referred to in the Question occurred some years ago. M. Montelar had been discovered by the Regent of Travancore to be engaged in some very questionable business; and having abused his influence, the Regent requested him to leave the territory. This step was concurred in by the Govern- ment of Madras as being essential to the welfare of Travancore.
