<Title>Housing of the Working Classes Bill</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 30 July 1885 vol 300 c522</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-07-30</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>117</WordCount>


<speaker>MR. GRAY</speaker> asked the President of the Local Government Board, Whether he will be prepared to extend the Housing of the Working Classes Bill to Ireland? 
<speaker>THE PRESIDENT (Mr. A. J. BALFOUR),</speaker> in reply, said, the Bill was not a Government Bill, and it was not in his charge, but in the charge of the Home Secretary. 
<speaker>THE SECRETARY OF STATE FOR THE HOME DEPARTMEMT (Sir R. ASSHETON CROSS)</speaker> said, the hon. Member would remember that the Commission which inquired into this matter, and on which he (Mr. Gray) took so important a part, did not inquire into the rural districts of Ireland. He did not know whether the Question of the hon. Member applied to towns.
