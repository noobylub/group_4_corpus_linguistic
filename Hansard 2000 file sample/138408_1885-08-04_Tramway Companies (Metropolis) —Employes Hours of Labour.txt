<Title>Tramway Companies (Metropolis) —Employes Hours of Labour</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 04 August 1885 vol 300 c1046</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-08-04</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>107</WordCount>


<speaker>MR. SAMUEL SMITH</speaker> asked the Secretary of State for the Home Department, If the attention of the Government has been called to the condition of labour imposed on the London tram men; if he is aware that they are compelled to work sixteen hours a-day, including Sundays; and, whether he will grant an inquiry into the subject? 
<speaker>THE SECRETARY OF STATE (Sir R. ASSHETON CROSS)</speaker> Yes, Sir; I have made inquiries, and I believe these men very often have to work 16 hours a-day. It seems to me enormous labour, as the House can understand, for we are ourselves working 16 hours a-day. We were so yesterday.
