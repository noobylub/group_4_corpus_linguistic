<Title>Navy—dockyard Expenditure</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 31 July 1885 vol 300 c671</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-07-31</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>113</WordCount>


<speaker>MR. GOURLEY</speaker> asked the First Lord of the Admiralty, To be good enough to inform the House if it is his intention to appoint a Departmental Committee to inquire into the present system of Dockyard expenditure; if so, whether he will consent to add to the Committee a few men independents the ordinary official routine of Admiralty work? 
<speaker>THE FIRST LORD OF THE ADMIRALTY (Lord GEORGE HAMILTON)</speaker> I stated yesterday the circumstances under which a Committee was appointed by the late Board of Admiralty and the nature of the inquiry they were directed to make; and until that inquiry is completed, it is not our intention to extend the scope of their investigations.
