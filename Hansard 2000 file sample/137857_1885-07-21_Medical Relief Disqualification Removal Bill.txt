<Title>Medical Relief Disqualification Removal Bill</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 21 July 1885 vol 299 c1408</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-07-21</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>176</WordCount>


<speaker>MR. PELL</speaker> asked the President of the Local Government Board, Whether, in the Medical Relief Disqualification Removal Bill , the term medical assistance is intended to cover what is generally known as medical extras—namely, wine, spirits, malt liquor, beef, and other butcher's meat; and, whether, in order to avoid misconception, he will in the Bill define what is meant by medical assistance? 
<speaker>THE PRESIDENT OF THE LOCAL GOVERNMENT BOARD (Mr. A. J. BALFOUR)</speaker> The term medical assistance is not intended to cover the medical extras referred to. The words used are the same as those in the Registration (Ireland) Act , and it does not appear to me to be necessary to introduce in the Bill any definition of the term. 
<speaker>MR. JESSE COLLINGS</speaker> Will the Bill not cover everything ordered by the medical officer, no matter what the articles may be? 
<speaker>MR. CAUSTON</speaker> Will the Bill cover surgical appliances? 
<speaker>THE PRESIDENT OF THE LOCAL GOVERNMENT BOARD</speaker> These are all matters to be debated in Committee. It is not convenient to enter into a discussion now.
