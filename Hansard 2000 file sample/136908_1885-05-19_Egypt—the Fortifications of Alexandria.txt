<Title>Egypt—the Fortifications of Alexandria</Title>
<DebRef>HC Deb 19 May 1885 vol 298 c944</DebRef>
<SittingDate>1885-05-19</SittingDate>
<SittingType>Commons</SittingType>
<WordCount>109</WordCount>


<speaker>SIR WALTER B. BARTTELOT</speaker> asked the Secretary of State for War, Whether any steps have been taken to restore the fortifications at Alexandria; and, if not, whether it is intended to take any steps to put that most important town in a proper state of defence? 
<speaker>SIR ARTHUR HAYTER</speaker> (who replied) said: A project for the repair of the defences of Alexandria has been submitted by the General Officer commanding in Egypt, and is now under the consideration of the Secretary of State. 
<speaker>SIR H. DRUMMOND WOLFF</speaker> Will the work be done at the expense of England or of Egypt? 
<speaker>SIR ARTHUR HAYTER</speaker> I am afraid I cannot say.
